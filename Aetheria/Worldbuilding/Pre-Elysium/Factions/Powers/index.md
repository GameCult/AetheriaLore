# Powers

*"Service, security, and sovereignty became the same invoice."*

The powers gathered here are the organizations that learned how to make infrastructure, law, custody, and necessity look like ordinary service provision. Some are megacorporations. Some are coordination regimes. Some survive by controlling a narrow choke point. All of them matter because they can decide what keeps moving, who gets recognized, and which crises are allowed to count as emergencies.

Read this branch as a map of institutional capacity rather than a popularity ranking. Major powers shape civilization directly, specialist firms weaponize dependency, and even dead organizations keep structuring the order through ruins, mergers, scandals, and inherited personnel.
