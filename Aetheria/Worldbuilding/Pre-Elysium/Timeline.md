### Overview

[[Colonizing Sol]] explains how corporate sovereignty replaced civic colonization. [[From Sol to Elysium]] explains why the whole solar order ultimately tried to outrun its own contradictions. The era notes below cover the long middle: who built the infrastructure, who captured it, who resisted, and which technologies changed the definition of personhood along the way.

### Era Sequence

[[Corporate Exodus]] - 2025-2100  
Privatization hardens into company sovereignty. The transport and extraction stack that will later become [[SolEx]] is assembled. Off-world debt peonage becomes normal.

[[Age of Automation]] - 2100-2250  
AGI, industrial genetics, and early cavity-habitat scaling produce enormous productivity gains and enormous new systems of control. The [[Pan-Solar Consortium]] emerges as a market stabilizer rather than a public government.

[[Identity Crisis]] - 2250-2500  
Uploading and uplift break the older boundaries of human personhood. Luxury immortality, industrial digital labor, and engineered sentience all become profitable at once.

[[Identity Abyss]] - 2500-2750  
Reality management, algorithmic governance, cognitive exploitation, and ideological hardening push Sol toward a society where most people live inside systems built to define them before they can define themselves.

[[Existential Collapse]] - 2750-3025  
Wireheading epidemics, resource contraction, insurgency, and elite exit politics make the old order visibly unsustainable. FTL stops being a frontier fantasy and becomes a desperation project.

[[FTL Trigger]] - March 17, 3025  
The last great technological gamble becomes the discontinuity between Sol and Elysium.
